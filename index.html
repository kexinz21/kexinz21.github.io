Hi there! I am Kexin (pronounced as `kuh-sheen').

I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, advised by Dr. Yuhang Zhao. My research interests are Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), accessibility, and augmented and virtual reality (AR/VR). I research and design intelligent interactive systems that support accessibility, inclusion, and safety for all. For now, my research focus on making social VR more inclusive and safe for people with disabilities.

I am an HCI researcher with interdisciplinary bakcground in Computer Science and Sociology, enabling me to approach problem spaces from a socio-technical perspective. Previously, I've received my master's degree in Information Science at Cornell University, where I worked with Dr. Andrea Stevenson Won and Shiri Azenkot on invisible disabilities representation and non-verbal communications in social VR. Before that, I earned a B.A. in Sociology and Economics from the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
